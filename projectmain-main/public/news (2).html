Latest News

Amazon's Approach to Carbon Credits

On February 14, 2025, Amazon detailed its strategy for utilizing high-quality carbon credits to combat climate change. The company focuses on reducing tropical forest loss, restoring degraded lands, and advancing CO₂ removal technologies. Each carbon credit represents one metric ton of CO₂ reduced or removed, linking financial investments to climate action beyond Amazon's direct operations.

Shell's Dominance in the Carbon Credit Market

In early February 2025, reports highlighted that Shell led the $1.4 billion global carbon credit market in 2024, surpassing other companies as the oil and gas sector scaled back clean energy investments. Shell retired 14.9 million credits, more than double that of its closest competitor, indicating a significant reliance on carbon offsets to meet climate targets

UN Approves Carbon Market Safeguards

In October 2024, the United Nations implemented mandatory safeguards in its new carbon market to prevent human rights violations and environmental damage by carbon credit project developers. This initiative aims to enhance the credibility and integrity of carbon offset projects globally.

Verra's Plan Raises Integrity Concerns

Also in October 2024, Verra, the world's largest certifier of carbon credits, announced plans to expedite project reviews despite reducing its workforce by 25% after a $9 million loss. This decision has raised concerns about the potential impact on the quality and integrity of carbon credit certifications.
